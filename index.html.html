üß© What is Linear Programming?

Linear Programming (LP) is a mathematical method used to find the best possible result (maximize or minimize) of a linear objective function subject to linear constraints (equations or inequalities).

üí° Why ‚ÄúLinear‚Äù and ‚ÄúProgramming‚Äù?

Linear: only first-degree terms (no exponents). Programming: not coding ‚Äî it means planning or selecting an optimal choice from alternatives.

‚öôÔ∏è Main Components of Linear Programming

A typical LP problem includes:

  • Decision Variables ‚Äî unknowns you control.
  • Constraints ‚Äî linear limits that must be satisfied.
  • Objective Function ‚Äî the linear formula to maximize or minimize.
  • Data ‚Äî the numerical coefficients and constants used in the model.

üìè Key Characteristics

Valid LPs must have:

  • Quantitative Constraints ‚Äî numeric limits.
  • Linearity ‚Äî no exponents or products of variables.
  • Objective Function ‚Äî linear expression to optimize.
  • Non-Negativity ‚Äî variables ‚â• 0.
  • Finiteness ‚Äî solutions and variables finite.

üßÆ Methods of Solving Linear Programming Problems

1. Graphical Method

Best for problems with two variables. Plot each constraint, identify the feasible region (intersection), then evaluate the objective at corner points to find the optimum.

2. Simplex Method

An algorithmic method for larger problems (more than two variables and many constraints). It traverses corner points of the feasible region in a structured way to locate the optimal vertex.

üåç Applications of Linear Programming

Business & Management

Used for production planning and profit maximization or cost minimization ‚Äî choose how many units of each product to make given limited resources.

Engineering

Optimize material use, machine schedules, or energy consumption subject to design and capacity constraints.

Transportation

Find shortest/cheapest routes, schedule deliveries, or allocate fleets to minimise time/fuel/costs.

Agriculture

Decide crop mix to maximise yield or profit given land, water, and labour restrictions.

Education

Used to create efficient class schedules, allocate teachers, and manage facilities.

Health & Nutrition

Plan diets that meet nutrition requirements at minimum cost using LP diet models.

üå± Why Linear Programming Matters

LP enables efficient use of limited resources, simplifies complex decision-making involving many variables, and provides clear, logical, optimal solutions for planning and resource allocation.

üßæ Summary

  • Linear Programming finds the best (maximum/minimum) value of a linear objective under linear constraints.
  • All relationships must be linear and variables are typically restricted to non-negative values.
  • For two-variable problems use the graphical method; for larger problems use the Simplex method.
  • LP is widely used in industry, engineering, transport, agriculture, education, and health.
